Why Get Vaccinated?

Vaccine has a long history to its invention and uses. One of the earliest types of vaccine created was believed to be in the 10th century in China. Instead of injecting the vaccine into the bloodstream, they blew the smallpox material to the human nostrils. After centuries, as the medical field had been tremendously improving, vaccination as of today is the act of injecting the vaccine into the vein. Inside vaccine is the weakened bacteria that caused the disease itself. By injecting the bacteria into the human’s body, the immune system will be able to recognize and analyze the disease, then program itself to prevent future possible encounters with the same disease. Due to its nature, there have been cases of fatality due to vaccination. Either the patient is allergic to the components of the vaccine, or have a weakened immune system. But those incidents rarely happen.

"To Be Vaccinated or Not To Be Vaccinated?"

While the idea of vaccination is helpful and practically important, many people argue that vaccines are toxic and dangerous to be put in a human’s body. Therefore, many organizations were founded in order to persuade people to stop getting vaccinated and rather have the immune system programmed naturally. According to Vaccine Liberation, “No vaccine on the market can honestly and accurately be said to have been proven both safe and effective” (“Questions and Answers”). This anti- vaccine organization argues that children’s immune systems are strong enough to fight off most infections naturally, and that having a vaccination not only weakens the body's natural defense system but also causes from minor to serious side effects. This organization is one of the most active groups that are creating the confusion in the society. Although the chances of having side effects are less than 1% for most vaccines, people are not sufficiently informed of the risks for the child as well as the society by not vaccinating.
